Haoqi Chen

I am a Master's student in Computer Science at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. I work with my advisor, Prof. Ely Kerman, on research at the intersection of symplectic geometry, optimization, and machine learning, with a focus on developing computational methods for mathematical modeling. Broadly, I am interested in applying AI and high-performance computing to complex systems, spanning from scientific simulations to large-scale information retrieval.

I have previously interned at the National Center for Supercomputing Applications, where I developed containerized HPC environments to streamline scientific workflows. I also have experience built multi-agent AI pipelines for international market intelligence and retrieval-augmented systems. I graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics and Computer Science, with Highest Distinction, from UIUC in 2025.
